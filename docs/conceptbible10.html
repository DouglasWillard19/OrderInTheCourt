Order in the Court - Concept Bible
FOREWORD – Conceptual Scope & Development Role This Concept Bible is intended to communicate the conceptual framework, systemic philosophy, and continuity boundaries of Order in the Court. It is not a production specification, a finalized technical design, or a locked implementation plan. Rather, it is a map of intent—describing how the world, systems, and roles of the game are meant to relate to one another at a foundational level. The ideas presented here are deliberately structural rather than prescriptive. They are meant to give experienced developers, designers, and engineers a clear understanding of what must remain coherent as the game evolves, while leaving ample room for iteration, optimization, and alternative implementations. This document assumes that the studio entrusted with building Order in the Court knows what it is doing, and it is written with respect for that expertise. Where questions arise—whether technical, systemic, or narrative—they are not only expected but welcomed. I am happy to discuss any aspect of this Concept Bible in detail. The purpose of those conversations is not to enforce rigidity, but to help resolve continuity questions, preserve the underlying philosophy, and ensure that the game remains internally consistent as it grows. During development, my intended role within the studio is that of Chief Justice. In this capacity, I serve as the steward of the game’s conceptual framework: its legal logic, systemic coherence, and narrative continuity. I am available to interpret this document, clarify intent, and help navigate design decisions that touch on the core identity of the project. That role, however, is advisory by design. While this Concept Bible reflects my voice and authority at the conceptual level, the Chief Justice does not possess decision-making power over production, implementation, scheduling, or final design choices unless explicitly invited to do so. Final authority rests with the studio and its designated leadership. Order in the Court is meant to be built collaboratively—balancing a strong, coherent vision with the practical realities and creative insights of the development team. This Concept Bible exists to support that collaboration, not to constrain it.

EXHIBIT C – Player Roles & Career Paths
C-1: Purpose of Player Roles - [Player roles define how individuals participate in the legal ecosystem of Order in the Court, establishing authority, responsibility, and professional context without locking players into permanent identities.]
C-2: Private Practice Context (PPP) - [The Private Practice Context encompasses roles operating in client-facing and firm-based environments, where professional success is shaped by reputation, relationships, and performance rather than institutional appointment.]
C-2.1: Paralegals (PPP) - [Paralegals in private practice support legal work through research, preparation, and procedural assistance, serving as an entry point into professional legal roles.]
C-2.2: Defense Attorneys - [Defense Attorneys represent clients within the adversarial system, operating under professional and ethical constraints while navigating risk, advocacy, and reputation.]
C-2.3: Managing Partners - [Managing Partners oversee firm operations and professional direction, balancing legal work with organizational leadership and strategic responsibility.]
C-2.4: Partners - [Partners represent senior private practitioners whose authority derives from experience, reputation, and firm standing, rather than formal appointment.]
C-3: Public Service Context (PSP) - [The Public Service Context encompasses roles operating within courts and public institutions, where authority is constrained by oversight, procedure, and institutional responsibility.]
C-3.1: Paralegals (PSP) - [Public Service Paralegals support institutional legal functions and court operations, assisting in procedural flow and administrative continuity.]
C-3.2: Prosecutors - [Prosecutors represent the public interest in criminal proceedings, exercising discretion within defined legal and ethical boundaries.]
C-3.3: Clerks - [Clerks support court operations and judicial workflow and may maintain professional affiliations that affect reputation or institutional prestige without exercising advocacy or judicial authority.]
C-3.4: Lower Court Judges - [Lower Court Judges preside over municipal, county, and federal district courts, exercising judicial authority within limited jurisdictional scope.]
C-3.5: State Appellate Court Judges - [State Appellate Court Judges review lower-court decisions, focusing on legal error, doctrine, and procedural correctness.]
C-3.6: State Supreme Court Justices - [State Supreme Court Justices exercise final authority over state law, shaping doctrine and precedent within their jurisdiction.]
C-3.7: Circuit Court Judges - [Circuit Court Judges preside over federal appellate matters, resolving conflicts and establishing binding precedent within their circuits.]
C-3.8: SCOTUS Justices - [SCOTUS Justices occupy the highest judicial authority, resolving constitutional questions and national legal conflicts.]
C-4: Movement Between Contexts - [Players are expected to move between Private Practice and Public Service contexts over the course of their careers, with professional history, reputation, and insight persisting across roles.]
C-5: Role Eligibility and Constraints - [Eligibility for roles is governed by accumulated reputation, connections, insight, and oversight systems, with specific promotion criteria and constraints intentionally left to studio implementation.]

C-6: Assignment and Authority Model - [Private Practice roles are selected and managed through player-driven and organizational decisions, while Public Service judicial roles are assigned through system-based selection, rotation, and term structures designed to balance access, authority, and experience.]
C-7: Baseline Model and Creative Control - [The role structures described in this exhibit represent a baseline conceptual model intended to support flexible career flow, with final implementation details and progression rules remaining under studio creative control.]

EXHIBIT F – Sidebar (Player Interface & Mediation Layer)
F-1: Purpose and Role - [Sidebar is the player-facing system interface through which all legal information, procedural options, and permissible actions are presented and mediated.]
F-2: Player Context Awareness - [Sidebar operates with awareness of the player’s role, court, case posture, and permissions, ensuring that only contextually valid actions and explanations are made available.]
F-3: Legal Translation and Guidance - [Sidebar translates legal doctrine and procedure into understandable guidance, presenting options and requirements without offering strategic judgment or advocacy.]
F-4: Insight and Permission Gating - [Sidebar regulates the depth and complexity of information available to a player based on Insight, expanding explanatory access without altering underlying mechanics or outcomes.]
F-5: Task Routing and System Invocation - [Sidebar determines what type of backend processing is required to satisfy a player request, defaulting to deterministic systems and escalating only when necessary.]
F-6: Relationship to IGLL - [All law presented by Sidebar is drawn exclusively from the In-Game Law Library (IGLL), including both controlling doctrine and historically retained dead law.]
F-7: Relationship to MARL0 - [Sidebar may invoke MARL0 for backend analysis or preparation when required, but all outputs are presented to the player solely through Sidebar without exposing MARL0 directly.]
F-8: Limits and Non-Authority - [Sidebar does not decide outcomes, practice law, exercise discretion, or act as an authority within the legal system.]
F-9: Canonical Behavior - [Sidebar operates in compliance with legal authority, functions neutrally within its constraints, and does not resist or interfere with the restoration of human judgment.]

EXHIBIT H – Law Systems
H-1: IRL Law Library (IRLLL)
H-1.1 - [The IRL Law Library (IRLLL) is a proprietary, real-world legal corpus developed and owned as Andyland intellectual property.]
H-1.2 - [IRLLL is designed as a reusable legal knowledge asset intended for licensing within the video game industry, including but not limited to its use in Order in the Court.]
H-1.3 - [IRLLL is licensed to the studio for use within Order in the Court and may be extended, by mutual agreement, for use in additional studio-developed titles. During this relationship, IRLLL is reserved for use with the studio within the gaming industry.]
H-1.4 - [IRLLL functions as an authoritative source of verified real-world legal text and doctrine, organized for structured access and adaptation into interactive systems.]
H-1.5 - [IRLLL is not a gameplay system and does not directly govern in-game outcomes.]
H-1.6 - [IRLLL is not directly accessible to players or player-facing systems.]
H-1.7 - [Access to IRLLL occurs exclusively through backend systems designed to retrieve, verify, structure, and synchronize legal material for in-game use.]
H-1.8 - [IRLLL is designed to operate in conjunction with supporting systems that enable its practical application within games, including backend intelligence and presentation layers.]
H-1.9 - [Only legal material that is intentionally selected, processed, and synchronized from IRLLL is introduced into the In-Game Law Library (IGLL).]
H-1.10 - [IRLLL evolves selectively; legal areas are constructed, reviewed, or updated only when invoked by gameplay needs or development objectives.]
H-1.11 - [IRLLL supplies authoritative grounding for in-game legal systems without dictating the evolution or outcomes of in-game law.]
H-2: In-Game Law Library (IGLL)
H-2.1 - Purpose and Authority
H-2.1a - [The In-Game Law Library (IGLL) is the authoritative body of law governing all legal reasoning, procedure, and outcomes within Order in the Court.]
H-2.1b - [IGLL represents the sovereign legal framework of the game world and is the sole source of controlling law for courts, judges, and legal actors.]
H-2.1c - [All judicial authority exercised within the game derives from IGLL and the precedential structures built upon it.]
H-2.2 - Initial Population (Seed Doctrine)
H-2.2a - [At launch, IGLL is prepopulated with a limited and curated set of legal provisions selected to support onboarding and early-stage gameplay.]
H-2.2b - [This initial body of law is referred to as Seed Doctrine.]
H-2.2c - [Seed Doctrine is sourced from the IRL Law Library (IRLLL) and intentionally introduced into IGLL prior to gameplay.]
H-2.2d - [Seed Doctrine exists to enable early defendant-focused cases without requiring the immediate construction of a comprehensive legal corpus.]
H-2.3 - Evolution Through Gameplay
H-2.3a - [After launch, IGLL evolves primarily through gameplay.]
H-2.3b - [Judicial decisions, appellate review, and higher-court rulings may refine, distinguish, expand, or overturn existing legal provisions within IGLL.]
H-2.3c - [As cases are adjudicated, the body of in-game law grows in depth and complexity in response to player action.]
H-2.3d - [Each server maintains its own evolving instance of IGLL, resulting in distinct legal ecosystems over time.]
H-2.4 - Precedent and Doctrinal Status
H-2.4a - [Within IGLL, precedent may be designated as binding or non-binding based on jurisdictional scope and court level.]
H-2.4b - [Lower courts are bound by controlling precedent issued by higher courts within the same jurisdictional hierarchy.]
H-2.4c - [Courts may distinguish existing precedent when materially different facts or controlling authority apply.]
H-2.4d - [Precedent may be overturned only through appropriate judicial processes consistent with court hierarchy.]
H-2.5 - Dead Law and Historical Record
H-2.5a - [Legal provisions or precedents that are overturned or superseded through gameplay remain recorded within IGLL.]
H-2.5b - [Such provisions are designated as non-controlling or “dead” law.]
H-2.5c - [Dead law is retained for historical reference, contextual understanding, and precedential analysis.]
H-2.5d - [IGLL does not retroactively erase legal history.]
H-2.6 - Relationship to Real-World Legal Change
H-2.6a - [Changes to real-world law do not automatically alter IGLL.]
H-2.6b - [Once introduced into IGLL, legal provisions remain governed by in-game processes.]
H-2.6c - [External legal developments affect IGLL only if the relevant legal area is reintroduced through gameplay and adjudication.]
H-2.6d - [This preserves continuity and stability within the game’s legal system.]
H-2.7 - Player-Facing Access Boundary
H-2.7a - [All player-facing legal information is drawn exclusively from IGLL.]
H-2.7b - [Players do not access IRLLL directly.]
H-2.7c - [The presentation and mediation of IGLL content to players occurs through player-facing systems defined elsewhere in this Concept Bible.]
H-2.8 - Scope and Limits
H-2.8a - [IGLL governs substantive law, procedural rules, and judicial precedent within the game.]
H-2.8b - [IGLL does not dictate player behavior outside legal contexts.]
H-2.8c - [IGLL functions as a system of record rather than an active decision-making entity.]
H-2.8d - [Systems that interpret, present, or operate upon IGLL are defined separately in this Concept Bible.]
